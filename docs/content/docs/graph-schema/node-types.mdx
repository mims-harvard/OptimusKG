OptimusKG contains 10 node types representing biomedical entities. Each node type is defined by its provenance from direct data sources and its mapping to standardized ontologies or controlled vocabularies.

## Entity Descriptions

### Gene (GEN)

Human genes identified by Ensembl gene IDs (e.g. `ENSG00000141510`). Consolidated from Open Targets, Bgee, and DisGeNET, with HGNC symbols as display names.

### Disease (DIS)

Disease entities harmonized across multiple ontologies including EFO, MONDO, Orphanet, DOID, HP, NCIT, and others. Sourced from Open Targets, DrugCentral, and Mondo.

### Biological Process (BPO)

Gene Ontology biological process terms describing ordered assemblies of molecular functions that achieve a biological objective.

### Phenotype (PHE)

Phenotypic abnormalities and observable traits, drawn from the Human Phenotype Ontology, MedDRA, and Open Targets.

### Drug (DRG)

Pharmaceutical compounds and therapeutic agents identified via ChEMBL, DrugBank, and RxNorm. Sourced from Open Targets, DrugBank, DrugCentral, and OnSIDES.

### Anatomy (ANA)

Anatomical structures from the UBERON cross-species ontology, covering tissues, organs, and other anatomical entities.

### Molecular Function (MFN)

Gene Ontology molecular function terms describing elemental activities of gene products at the molecular level.

### Cellular Component (CCO)

Gene Ontology cellular component terms describing locations relative to cellular structures where gene products perform their functions.

### Pathway (PWY)

Biological pathways from Reactome and Open Targets, capturing curated signaling, metabolic, and regulatory pathway descriptions.

### Exposure (EXP)

Environmental exposures and chemicals sourced from the Comparative Toxicogenomics Database (CTD), mapped to MeSH terms.
